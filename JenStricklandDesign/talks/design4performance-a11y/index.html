Design for Performance & Accessibility

By Jen Strickland

When do you think about performance and accessibility?

  • Many teams optimize performance and add accessibility accommodations in the development phase — after stakeholder sign-off.
  • At this point, the dev team is on the hook for design decisions which may not optimally balance performance and aesthetics, nor consider the experience for users of assistive technology.

Accessibility & Performance? Really?

  • In my experience… this presentation is based on my nearly two decades of online design.
  • Marcy Sutton actually quantified it, in presentations and a blog post.

Accessibility & Performance? Really?

TL;DR

  • People rely on keyboards and AT to interact with the web. Provide basic accessibility support, before tuning performance. Start there.
  • Every repaint introduces difficulty for accessibility…
    and a performance hit.
  • The longer it takes to load and render a site, the delay is even greater for AT users, so optimize assets.

Accessibility & Performance? Really?

Bonus: it’s more efficient and cost-effective to have accessibility figured out before development or launch, opposed to hacking add-on’s or addressing a lawsuit.

A great experience is performant, usable, and ought to be so for all.

Shift Left: Prioritize Performance & Accessibility from the Start

  • Business requirements include performance and accessibility
  • Indicate metrics for launch
  • Design experience with performance and accessibility as priorities

Shift Left: A Process

This talk outlines a design process prioritizing performance and accessibility from the beginning of your project, including:

  • “Design in the browser” methodology
  • QA for accessibility and performance throughout
  • Specs provided for design and accessibility
  • Test constantly — can’t stress this enough

Shift Left: Getting Started

QA for accessibility and performance throughout

  • Validate design using accessibility tools
  • Weigh the aesthetic and performance value
  • Test constantly, on many devices, using a variety of connections
  • Use tools to evaluate design decision impact
