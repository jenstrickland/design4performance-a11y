Prioritize Performance & Accessibility at the Beginning

Too often projects optimize performance and add-on accessibility in the development phase. Design decisions might make it necessary to bloat (read slow) a project with extra JavaScript to make a design accessible, creating a less than optimal user experience, due to lesser performance and difficulty for those using assistive tech.

This design process leverages a "design in the browser" methodology, incorporating accessible solutions, and QA'ing for accessibility and performance at regular intervals. Marcy Sutton wrote about how sites with performance issues are often nightmares for accessibility in her post, Accessibility and Performance.
